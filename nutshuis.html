Sacha van Alfen
Stadsbeestjes
Most people either don’t notice them, swat them away or cruelly stamp on them: insects. But these tiny creatures actually deserve our praises or even a medal. Without insects, many of our crops wouldn’t thrive. Without insects, the soil would be infertile. In fact without insects, would there even be life? Nobody knows the answer to this question, but one thing is patently clear: insects are vital – including in our cities, and yes, also in The Hague. Together with six other graphic design students from the KABK - Isabella Giacomelli, Eva Maria Horvath, Fien Leeflang, Denise Jansen and Laura Brouwer - we made four interactive installations for the exhibition called City Bugs in Het Nutshuis. Through text, image and assignments you can playfully discover how indispensable insects are in our city.

ABOUT

Hi! My name is Sacha van Alfen.
I am a Graphic Designer who recently graduated from the Royal Academy of Arts with a great interest in (motion) typography, print and coding. I like to work digital as much as I like to work with print. Projects I work on vary from a chatbot about the two faces of nostalgia, a website about a stamp collection published in Iran during the Iran - Iraq war, a publication about the importance of the bee, a game about the ecological footprint of animal agricult and more.

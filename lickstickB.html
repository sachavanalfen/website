LICK'EM STICK'EM
A semester-long collaboration between the 3rd year bachelor of Graphic Design class at the Royal Academy of Art, The Hague and The International Institute of Social History (IISH) in Amsterdam. We as 3rd year students researched long-term archival approaches and experimented with digital media-transcoding based on original cultural artefacts from the IISH. The artefact I chose from the IISG is a collection of 114 stamps published in Iran during the Iran - Iraq war. The book I designed is in a way an analogue version of the website I made which you can find here.

About
ABOUT

Hi! My name is Sacha van Alfen.
I am a Graphic Designer who recently graduated from the Royal Academy of Arts with a great interest in (motion) typography, print and coding. I like to work digital as much as I like to work with print. Projects I work on vary from a chatbot about the two faces of nostalgia, a website about a stamp collection published in Iran during the Iran - Iraq war, a publication about the importance of the bee, a game about the ecological footprint of animal agricult and more.

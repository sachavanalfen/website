How it Really Happened
My teachers copy of the New Larousse Encyclopedia of Mythology he bought before summer was falling apart. He asked us if we, students of the third year, could give his book a quick fix. And while we were at it, we had to… Question it, Edit it, Update it, Re-write it, Reproduce it, Distribute it. We all had to choose twelve consecutive pages and take some time to read up on the content. The assignment called for an intervention: an image or text takeover initiated by us, inspired by the books original content. In the end we collected all the digital files and combined them again in to a new book. A re-written, reproduced, updated, distributed version of the original Laurousse Encyclopedia of Mythology. For my part of the book I though of a system to give new meaning to the stories being told. I took sentences from the pages I chose, and created a new story line from them. The underlined sentences and page numbers function as a system to re-locate where the sentences originate from.
